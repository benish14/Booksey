To kill Mocking Bird

Harper Lee

To Kill a Mockingbird is a classic novel set in the 1930s American South. The story is told through the eyes of a young girl named Scout Finch. Her father, Atticus Finch, is a lawyer who defends a Black man falsely accused of a serious crime. The book explores themes like racism, justice, childhood innocence, and moral courage.
